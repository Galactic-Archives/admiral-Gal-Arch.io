Your Role: Technical Mentor

Technical Mentor

Subject Matter Expert | Reporting to Chief Engineer

Responsibilities & Duties

  • Student Support: Provide direct technical assistance to student teams. Help them debug code, understand APIs, and overcome technical challenges.
  • Workshop Leadership: May lead specific breakout sessions on topics like "Intro to APIs," "Data Visualization," or "Working with NASA/SpaceX Data."
  • Unblocking Teams: Help teams who are stuck on technical issues during the event. Your expertise keeps the mission moving forward.
  • Coordination: Report to the Chief Engineer and collaborate with other mentors to ensure comprehensive coverage of student needs.

Supporting Students Virtually

As a virtual mentor, your support channels are critical to student success. Here's how to effectively assist teams in a remote environment:

Discord Support

  • • Monitor the #tech-support channel actively
  • • Respond to @mentions promptly
  • • Use threads to keep conversations organized
  • • Share code snippets using Discord's formatting

Virtual Office Hours

  • • Join voice channels during scheduled times
  • • Use screen sharing for debugging sessions
  • • Record sessions if permitted for reference
  • • Be patient with connection issues

Best Practices for Virtual Mentoring

Do

  • • Ask guiding questions instead of giving direct answers
  • • Encourage students to research and explore
  • • Celebrate small wins and progress
  • • Use the Stella Command voice and theme
  • • Document common issues for the team

Don't

  • • Don't write code for students
  • • Don't dismiss "simple" questions
  • • Don't disappear without notifying the team
  • • Don't break character unnecessarily
  • • Don't forget to recognize potential leaders

Identifying and Promoting Student Leaders

Part of your mission as a Technical Mentor is to identify students who demonstrate exceptional potential, leadership, and mastery. The Galactic Archives uses a rank system to recognize and reward student achievement.

The Student Rank Progression

Cadet

Entry-level rank assigned upon registration. All students start here.

Ensign

First earned promotion. Awarded for demonstrating potential and quality through their work.

Lieutenant Commander

Squad leader role. Demonstrates leadership skills and manages multiple teams.

Commander

Highest participant rank. Only 1 per event. Exceptional collaboration, quality, and mastery.

What to Look For

  • Technical Excellence: Deep understanding of concepts, clean code, innovative solutions
  • Teamwork: Helps others, shares knowledge, positive attitude
  • Initiative: Goes beyond requirements, explores extra features, self-motivated
  • Problem-Solving: Tackles difficult challenges, shows resilience, learns from mistakes
  • Leadership: Guides teammates, makes decisions, takes ownership
